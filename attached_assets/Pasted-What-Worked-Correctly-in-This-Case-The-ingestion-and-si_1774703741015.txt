What Still Needs Refinement

Two things are visible in this screen.

1) External signals are mixed with internal signals

You currently have:

Regulatory / industry news
Operational readiness signals
Internal execution constraints

All in the same list.

That reduces clarity for decision-making.

They should be separated.

The correct structure

Primary Signals
→ internal controllable drivers

External Signals
→ environment changes outside your control

Missing Signals
→ unknowns that determine risk

In this case, the classification should look like this
Primary Signals (Internal)

Field force expansion only 40% complete
Manufacturing dependent on single supplier
Launch readiness constrained by staffing
Supply vulnerability to disruption

External Signals

Medicaid prior authorization requirements emerging
Competitor real-world adherence data expected
Regulatory progress in therapeutic area

Missing Signals

Final payer coverage decisions
Completion date for field force hiring
Secondary supplier approval status
Initial prescribing trend in first 60 days

That separation will make the system immediately clearer for executives.

2) Importance weighting is still conservative

Several signals marked Low importance are actually High leverage.

Specifically:

Field force only 40% complete
Single supplier dependency
Payer restrictions

These are not minor signals.

They are primary adoption constraints.

Correct importance calibration

Field force readiness
High

Supply reliability
High

Payer access restrictions
High

Competitor evidence
Medium

Investor conference presentation
Low

This is a calibration issue, not a logic issue.
