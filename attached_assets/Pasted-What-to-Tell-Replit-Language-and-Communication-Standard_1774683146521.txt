What to Tell Replit — Language and Communication Standard

From now on, all user-facing outputs in CIOS must use clear, direct, executive language.
The system should communicate like a senior pharmaceutical leader explaining a situation to a team — not like a statistician, data scientist, or technical model.

Avoid mathematical or technical wording unless explicitly requested in advanced detail mode.

The goal is clarity, judgment, and action — not explanation of calculations.

The Communication Model the System Must Follow

Every output should answer four questions immediately:

What is most likely?
Why do we think that?
What should we do now?
What would change the situation?

If the output does not clearly answer those four questions, it is incomplete.

Replace Technical Language with Executive Language

Tell Replit to enforce these substitutions.

Do not say:

Say instead:

How the situation is evolving

The System Must Sound Like an Executive, Not an Analyst

Correct tone:

We see no readiness signals yet.
Historically, that pattern leads to delays.
Plan for a later launch unless new evidence emerges.

Incorrect tone:

The forecast reflects unresolved constraints across operational readiness domains, resulting in a capped probability ceiling.

Confidence Language Standard

Confidence should always be expressed in words, not numbers.

Allowed:

High
Moderate
Low

Optional clarification:

High — strong and consistent signals
Moderate — mixed or incomplete signals
Low — limited or conflicting evidence

UI Rule — Always Lead With the Answer

The first line on the screen should always be:

Fallback Rule — When Evidence Is Thin

The system should never say:

Instead say:

There is not enough evidence yet to support a confident decision.
Based on current signals, delay remains the safer assumption.

That preserves authority.
