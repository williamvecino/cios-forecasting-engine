CIOS Feedback Package — Case 2
Case

Donanemab FDA approval by July 2024

Core diagnosis from this case

This case showed that CIOS can reason much better about a regulatory approval decision than it did in the first case. The engine surfaced the right core gates:

ARIA safety concerns
Advisory committee recommendation
Clinical data support
Priority review

So the underlying reasoning is improving.

The main remaining issue is now more specific:

The reasoning layer is increasingly correct, but the interface, template routing, weighting, and output framing are still inconsistent.

This case exposed four main problem clusters:

Signal dependency and weighting are still wrong
Negative signals are undercounted or neutralized
Commercial/adoption language still leaks into regulatory cases
UI/template routing is inconsistent across steps
Sequential feedback by step
Step 3 — Add Information
What worked

The initial signal set was appropriate for a regulatory approval case:

TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 efficacy
FDA priority review
Advisory committee scheduling
subgroup safety/efficacy
ARIA risk
Issues to fix
1. Negative regulatory risk was initially underrepresented

The system initially behaved as if the case were mostly positive and low-friction.

Fix request

For regulatory approval cases, require at least one explicit downward-pressure safety or risk signal whenever a known major safety concern exists.

Examples for this case:

ARIA safety concern
benefit vs risk uncertainty
advisory risk due to safety debate
Expected behavior

Regulatory cases should not summarize as “strong positive / no negatives” when known safety controversy exists.

2. ARIA was initially treated too softly

ARIA was initially closer to “missing/neutral” than a real barrier.

Fix request

In regulatory cases, known safety controversies should default to:

negative or constraining
not neutral
included in downward pressure unless explicitly cleared
Expected behavior

Known safety issues should automatically register as real gating constraints.

3. Signal dependency is still not properly compressed

The system treated several related items as separate strong supports:

TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 efficacy
subgroup analysis
advisory scheduling
trial-linked supportive evidence

These are not fully independent.

Fix request

Improve dependency compression for regulatory cases.

Required behavior:

link trial outcome, subgroup analysis, and derivative evidence under shared RootEvidenceID
do not let each derivative support add full positive weight
show compressed evidence families before Judge
Expected behavior

A trial program should contribute as a cluster, not as multiple full-strength independent signals.

Step 4 — Judge
What worked

The Judge step did much better here than in the first case:

approval likely: 70%
ARIA safety as main constraint
advisory committee as gating factor

This is directionally correct.

Issues to fix
1. Commercial language still appeared inside a regulatory case

Examples:

operational readiness
market ready to deliver
execution gap

These are not the right framing for FDA approval probability.

Fix request

Add case-type-specific vocabulary control for Judge.

For regulatory cases, allowed framing should favor:

benefit–risk balance
regulatory tolerance
safety acceptability
advisory uncertainty
label constraints
review dynamics

Disallowed or deprioritized framing:

market readiness
operational readiness
adoption ceiling
execution gap
Expected behavior

Judge should speak in regulatory language if the case is regulatory.

2. Negative signal contribution was still broken

The negative signal around clinical benefit vs safety risk contributed 0 pts and ended up effectively neutral.

That is incorrect.

Fix request

Fix downward pressure scoring so genuine negative regulatory signals always contribute non-zero downward force unless explicitly overruled by stronger clearing evidence.

Expected behavior

If ARIA or benefit-risk concern exists, total downward pressure cannot be zero.

3. Confidence state was inconsistent

The screen simultaneously suggested:

high confidence
moderate confidence
mixed/incomplete signals

These are contradictory.

Fix request

Refactor confidence display so there is one primary confidence state, with optional subcomponents.

Recommended structure:

Forecast Confidence
Evidence Confidence
Constraint Uncertainty

But only one top-line confidence label should appear.

Expected behavior

No contradictory confidence labels in the same case state.

4. Historical analogs were low relevance

The analog set shown included low-relevance categories like rare disease, respiratory/chronic disease, oncology.

Fix request

Improve analog retrieval for regulatory cases so analogs are matched primarily on:

approval decision type
safety controversy type
therapeutic area relevance
advisory committee relevance
mechanism/class similarity
Expected behavior

A neurodegenerative regulatory case should not default to generic low-relevance analogs.

Step 5 — Decide
What worked

The barrier decomposition itself was largely good:

ARIA incidence monitoring
risk mitigation strategies
subgroup safety analysis
regulatory precedent
advisory committee benchmarks

That logic is appropriate.

Issues to fix
1. Adoption language leaked into a regulatory case

Examples:

adoption ceiling
accelerate adoption
stakeholder hesitation
market readiness
Fix request

In regulatory cases, the Decide layer should use:

approval leverage
review leverage
safety resolution leverage
advisory leverage

Not adoption framing.

Expected behavior

The action layer should be regulatory, not launch-oriented.

2. Segment assignments were wrong for this case type

The Decide screen showed commercial segments like:

community neurologists
hospitals
primary care physicians
skeptical specialists

These are launch/adoption actors, not approval actors.

Fix request

Make Step 5 segmentation case-type specific.

For regulatory approval cases, allowed actor groups should be things like:

FDA review division
advisory committee members
company regulatory/clinical team
safety reviewers
patient advocacy/public voice if relevant
Expected behavior

Commercial prescriber segmentation should not appear in a regulatory approval case.

3. Competitive risk was framed as market competition

Examples:

fast follower risk
incumbent defense
access response

These are commercialization risks.

Fix request

For regulatory cases, replace competitive risk with regulatory precedent / class risk.

Examples:

prior class approval/rejection pattern
mechanism-specific safety spillover
comparative review tolerance
precedent from similar amyloid-targeting therapies
Expected behavior

Competitive module should be routed by case type.

4. Growth feasibility should not appear in this case type

Segment size, scalability, revenue, access expansion do not belong in an FDA approval decision workflow.

Fix request

Hide Growth Feasibility entirely for regulatory cases.

Expected behavior

Only show this module for commercial/adoption/market expansion cases.

Step 6 — Respond
What worked

This was better than the first case. The response correctly centered:

approval likely
ARIA as key risk
advisory committee as pivotal

Priority actions were mostly sensible:

finalize ARIA risk mitigation
engage advisory committee
complete subgroup analyses
protect timeline
Issues to fix
1. Commercialization language still leaked in

Examples:

market positioning against competitors
slow adoption among cautious prescribers
future rollout
market readiness
Fix request

Apply case-type aware response templates.

For regulatory cases, the response should center on:

approval path
approval risk
review milestones
regulatory contingency plan
label / safety implications
Expected behavior

Respond should not drift into launch strategy unless the case is adoption-focused.

2. Physician education was listed as a pre-approval action

“Prepare targeted physician education materials” is not a core approval driver.

Fix request

Restrict pre-approval action recommendations to regulatory-relevant tasks.

Allowed examples:

advisory preparation
FDA briefing response
safety protocol refinement
subgroup/statistical clarification
risk mitigation submission readiness
Expected behavior

Post-approval education tasks should not be framed as approval-driving actions.

3. Success measures mixed approval milestones with launch readiness

Examples included:

broader real-world rollout
market readiness
Fix request

For regulatory cases, success measures should be limited to:

advisory committee outcome
FDA action outcome
safety acceptance
label scope
risk management acceptance
Expected behavior

Success metrics should remain inside the case’s decision domain.

4. Health economics was overused as approval support

Economic burden and favorable health economics appeared as if they supported FDA approval.

Fix request

Reduce or block HEOR/economic arguments from materially influencing regulatory approval probability unless the case type is payer/access.

Expected behavior

Health economics may be visible as contextual background, but not a core approval driver.

Cross-case architectural fixes reinforced by this case

This case strongly supports these broader product changes:

1. Case-type classifier must control every downstream module

This case makes it clear that one part of the system knows it is regulatory, while other parts still think it is commercialization/adoption.

Fix request

Use case type to explicitly route:

vocabulary
driver library
gates
action modules
segmentation
simulation labeling
visible UI sections
2. Hide irrelevant modules by case type

This case should not show:

Growth Feasibility
launch personas
adoption ceiling language
market readiness metrics
Fix request

Implement case-type-specific visible modules.

3. Downward pressure must not collapse to zero in risk-heavy regulatory cases

This happened in Judge and affected the case realism.

Fix request

Add a safeguard:
If a case contains unresolved safety gate + explicit negative signal, total downward pressure cannot be zero.

4. Improve output layer consistency

This case showed:

good reasoning in some steps
wrong framing in others
correct actors in simulation but wrong personas still visible
Fix request

Audit template routing across steps to ensure one case type remains active across the entire workflow.

Short Replit-ready version

We just completed a Donanemab FDA approval test case. The reasoning engine is stronger than before and identifies the right regulatory gates, but several interface/template/weighting issues remain.

Please fix the following:

Make case-type routing persistent across the entire workflow. This is a regulatory approval case, but commercial/adoption language and modules still appear in Judge, Decide, Respond, and Simulate.
Remove commercialization framing from regulatory cases. Replace terms like operational readiness, market readiness, adoption ceiling, future rollout, and execution gap with regulatory terms like benefit-risk balance, safety acceptability, review leverage, and approval path.
Fix negative signal scoring. Known safety risks like ARIA and benefit-vs-risk uncertainty should contribute real downward pressure and must not collapse to 0 pts.
Improve dependency compression. TRAILBLAZER-ALZ2 efficacy, subgroup analysis, and related derivative evidence should not all count as fully independent strong supports.
Unify confidence display. Do not show contradictory high vs moderate confidence states in the same screen.
In Decide, replace commercial prescriber segmentation with regulatory actors for regulatory cases.
Hide commercial modules like Growth Feasibility and market/launch segmentation in regulatory cases.
In Respond, keep actions strictly pre-approval and regulatory. Remove physician education or other post-approval commercialization tasks from approval-driving recommendations.
In Respond, success measures should be regulatory milestones only, not launch readiness or rollout markers.
Rename Simulate by case type. “Simulate Adoption Reaction” should not appear in regulatory cases.
Remove adoption personas from the regulatory simulation screen and keep only regulatory actors.
Reclassify Patient Advocacy Groups as potentially pre-approval actors in regulatory cases.
Improve historical analog selection so regulatory neurodegenerative cases match to more relevant approval analogs.

That set of changes should make regulatory cases much cleaner and more internally consistent.
