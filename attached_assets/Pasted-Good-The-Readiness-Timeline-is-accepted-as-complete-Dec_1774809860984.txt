Good. The Readiness Timeline is accepted as complete.

## Decision

Proceed to the next canonical build step: **Competitive Risk Panel**.

## Purpose

Move from readiness and adoption timing to **what competitive forces could prevent, delay, compress, or reverse adoption**.

This panel must identify where the forecast is vulnerable to competitor behavior, incumbent strength, category crowding, displacement risk, and strategic counter-moves.

## What this panel must answer

At minimum:

* What is the biggest competitive risk to adoption?
* Is the current forecast vulnerable to incumbency, competitor differentiation, timing, access, or commercial execution?
* Which segments are most exposed to competitive pressure?
* Which competitor dynamics could stall or reverse readiness?
* Which competitive risks are structural versus emerging?
* What risk should be monitored first?

## Build requirements

### 1) Define competitive risk schema

Create a structured risk object that can support at minimum:

* `CompetitiveRiskID`
* `CaseID`
* `SegmentID` (nullable for overall risk)
* `RiskName`
* `RiskCategory`
* `RiskStrength`
* `RiskConfidence`
* `RiskScope`
* `PrimarySignals`
* `CounterSignals`
* `ThreatMechanism`
* `WhyItMatters`
* `StructuralVsEmerging`
* `EstimatedForecastImpact`
* `PriorityRank`
* `RationaleSummary`

### 2) Initial competitive risk taxonomy

Support a practical first-pass taxonomy grounded in pharma forecasting logic.

Examples:

* Incumbent Entrenchment
* Superior Competitor Differentiation
* Guideline Lockout / Competitor First-Mover Advantage
* Access / Formulary Disadvantage
* Commercial Field Force Disadvantage
* KOL / Advocacy Capture
* Workflow Preference for Existing Standard
* Category Crowding / Confusion
* Competitor Evidence Acceleration
* Pricing / Contracting Pressure
* Channel / Account Control
* Switching Inertia

These categories must be explicit and consistent.

### 3) Overall + segment-level risk diagnosis

The panel should support:

* overall competitive risks for the case
* segment-level competitive risk views

I want to see whether one segment is mainly exposed to switching inertia while another is exposed to access disadvantage or KOL capture.

### 4) Output required for each risk

For each competitive risk show:

* risk category
* risk strength
* confidence
* threat mechanism
* key supporting signals
* key counter-signals
* why this risk matters
* whether it is structural or emerging
* estimated impact on adoption / readiness / segment movement

### 5) Relationship to prior layers

This panel must be derived from:

* forecast outputs
* adoption segments
* barrier diagnosis
* readiness timeline
* dependency-controlled signals
* relevant reference patterns where appropriate

Do not make it a generic competitor profile page. It must remain tied to forecast vulnerability.

### 6) Priority logic

Add a ranking framework that distinguishes:

* high-impact structural risks
* high-impact emerging risks
* segment-specific risks
* watch-list risks that are not yet active but could escalate
* risks that are mostly downstream echoes and should rank lower

The panel should help answer:
**Which competitive risk is most likely to damage adoption if left unaddressed?**

### 7) UI requirements

Need a usable panel that shows:

* ranked overall competitive risks
* risk distribution by category
* segment-level competitive risk view
* structural vs emerging markers
* drilldown with supporting/counter-signals and rationale

Functional first, polished later.

### 8) Guardrail

Do not let this become a simple competitor summary.

Competitive Risk must remain tied to:

* forecast vulnerability
* segment exposure
* readiness disruption
* explicit signals and mechanisms

## Deliverables

Return with:

1. competitive risk schema
2. risk taxonomy
3. overall competitive risk ranking working
4. segment-level competitive risk diagnosis working
5. priority / structural-vs-emerging logic working
6. usable panel UI
7. clear note on how competitive risk outputs are derived from forecast + segment + barrier + readiness logic
