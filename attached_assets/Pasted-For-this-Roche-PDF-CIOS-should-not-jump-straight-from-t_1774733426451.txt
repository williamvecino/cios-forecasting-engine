For this Roche PDF, CIOS should not jump straight from the upload to one giant question. The document is an agency RFP and it explicitly asks for a written proposal and, if invited, a live pitch. It asks agencies to provide preliminary pre-launch and launch strategies for patients and caregivers, differentiate anti-PDL1 from anti-PD1, propose a market research plan, and recommend up to 3 engagement programs. It also specifies live-pitch requirements around strategy, disease education, and creative concepts.

So the problem is not that CIOS is “bad at forecasting.” The problem is that it needs an upstream AI brief-interpreter agent that reads the PDF the way an experienced strategist would and converts it into a structured decision pack before CIOS does anything.

The current question in your screenshot — “What is the optimal launch strategy and brand positioning…” — compresses too many distinct asks into one mega-question. The RFP actually contains multiple decision threads: the business context and launch timing for UBC and NSCLC, the patient/caregiver strategy requirement, the differentiation requirement versus PD-1, the market-research requirement, the engagement-program requirement, and the agency evaluation criteria.

What CIOS should do instead is this:

Step 1: read the document and classify the real decision
For this PDF, the underlying decision is not just “brand strategy.” It is closer to:
How should an agency respond to Genentech’s anti-PDL1 RFP with a patient/caregiver pre-launch and launch strategy for UBC and NSCLC, in a competitive PD-1/PD-L1 environment?
That is grounded in the RFP’s description of the agency search and the proposal requirements.

Step 2: split the document into a decision pack
The agent should output:

Primary decision
Secondary decisions
Required strategic outputs
Required tactical outputs
Evaluation criteria
Missing information
Recommended CIOS questions

For this RFP, those recommended CIOS questions would be more like:

What are the biggest launch barriers for anti-PDL1 in UBC?
What are the biggest launch barriers for anti-PDL1 in NSCLC?
How should anti-PDL1 be differentiated from anti-PD1 for patients and caregivers?
Which pre-launch disease-education program is most likely to create advantage?
Which patient/caregiver channels are highest value by indication?
What market-research inputs are essential before final positioning?

Those questions map directly to the written proposal and live-pitch asks in the RFP.

Step 3: only then send structured questions into CIOS
CIOS is better at answering bounded questions than absorbing a 12-page RFP and inventing the right framing.

So yes: there should be a general AI agent ahead of CIOS.

Call it something like:

Document Interpreter Agent
or
RFP / Brief Interpreter

Its job is:

recover content from the PDF
classify the underlying business decision
extract required outputs
detect explicit asks
identify missing assumptions
generate 3–10 coherent CIOS questions
optionally prefill a case shell

That would make CIOS feel much closer to how I reason over the PDF.
