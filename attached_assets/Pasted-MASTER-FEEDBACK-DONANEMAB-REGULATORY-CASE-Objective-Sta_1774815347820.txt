MASTER FEEDBACK — DONANEMAB REGULATORY CASE

Objective: Stabilize causal logic, decision-layer separation, and actor behavior modeling for regulatory approval workflows.

This document consolidates all required fixes identified during the case walkthrough.

SECTION 1 — Core System Architecture Fixes
1. Enforce Sequential Decision Layer Separation

The system currently mixes:

Regulatory approval
Reimbursement / HTA
Adoption

These must be separated into explicit decision layers.

Required architecture

Layer 1
Regulatory approval

Layer 2
HTA / reimbursement

Layer 3
Adoption / utilization

Required behavior

Regulatory approval must be determined only by:

clinical efficacy
safety profile
regulatory compliance
data integrity
risk mitigation

Reimbursement must be determined by:

cost-effectiveness
budget impact
pricing

Adoption must be determined by:

physician behavior
infrastructure readiness
patient acceptance
Required rule

Cost-effectiveness constraints must never appear in:

Regulatory bottlenecks

They belong only in:

Access constraints
Reimbursement constraints

2. Remove Circular Drivers

The system currently allows:

Regulatory approval by EMA
Regulatory approval by FDA

to appear as drivers.

These are outcomes, not causes.

Required rule

If:

VariableType = DecisionOutcome

Then:

Disallow placement in:

Drivers
Signals
Constraints

Replace with

Regulatory readiness
Submission completeness
Evidence sufficiency
Safety resolution

SECTION 2 — Signal and Evidence Logic Fixes
3. Safety vs Efficacy Must Always Exert Downward Pressure

A high-importance safety signal currently produces:

Total Downward Pressure: 0

This is incorrect.

Required rule

If:

SignalImportance = High
AND SignalDirection = Negative or Constraining
AND GateStatus ≠ Resolved

Then:

DownwardPressure must be greater than zero

Expected behavior

High unresolved safety risk must always reduce forecast probability.

4. Reclassify Safety vs Efficacy Signal

Currently placed under:

Neutral

This is incorrect.

Required classification

Safety vs efficacy must be:

Constraining
or
Mixed

Never:

Neutral

5. Separate Regulatory Drivers from Market Access Drivers

Health economics currently appears as a driver of approval.

This is incorrect.

Required rule

If:

DecisionAuthority = FDA or EMA

Then:

Primary drivers must be limited to:

clinical efficacy
safety profile
trial integrity
regulatory compliance

Health economics must be classified as:

Downstream impact

7. Decouple Regulatory Authorization from Market Readiness

Current behavior:

Approval automatically implies market readiness.

This is incorrect.

Required rule

Regulatory authorization

Market readiness requires:

Manufacturing
Distribution
Coverage
Implementation

This is incorrect.

Forecast ceiling must be constrained regardless of other progress.
