The Cases That Do NOT Fit the Five

These are not edge cases.
They are different decision mechanics.

6) Investment / Portfolio Decision Archetype
Example: Continue vs terminate an asset
Typical question

Should a company continue funding development of a drug after a Phase 2 readout?

Why this doesn't fit the five

This decision is not about:

launch timing
adoption
regulation
access
competition

It is about:

capital allocation under uncertainty

What drives this case
probability of technical success
market size
cost of development
expected return
strategic fit
risk tolerance
Real example

Company decides whether to continue development of a new oncology therapy after mixed Phase 2 results.

This is a portfolio decision.

Signals look like
trial efficacy magnitude
safety risk
development cost escalation
commercial opportunity size
competitor pipeline progress
internal portfolio priorities
Core judgment question

Is continued investment economically justified?

7) Operational Execution / Supply Disruption Archetype
Example: Manufacturing outage risk
Typical question

Will supply shortages occur due to manufacturing disruption?

Why this doesn't fit the five

This is not a launch or regulatory question.

It is:

operational continuity risk

What drives this case
plant shutdown
inspection finding
batch failure
capacity constraint
supply chain interruption
Real example

Will a sterile manufacturing issue cause drug shortages in the next 3 months?

Signals look like
FDA inspection observation
production delay
capacity loss
inventory depletion
supplier disruption
Core judgment question

Can supply be maintained?

8) Strategic Partnership / M&A Archetype
Example: Acquisition or licensing decision
Typical question

Will Company A acquire Company B or license a specific asset?

Why this doesn't fit the five

This is not a clinical or commercial outcome.

It is:

corporate strategy execution

What drives this case
valuation
pipeline fit
financial capacity
negotiation progress
competitive interest
Real example

Will a large pharmaceutical company acquire a biotech developing a new therapy?

Signals look like
partnership discussions
financing activity
strategic announcements
board-level decisions
competitor bids
Core judgment question

Will the deal happen?

9) Policy / Environment Shift Archetype
Example: Regulatory or reimbursement environment change
Typical question

Will a new national policy change reimbursement rules?

Why this doesn't fit the five

This is not product-specific.

It is:

system-level environment change

What drives this case
legislation
rulemaking
government policy
budget constraints
political pressure
Real example

Will Medicare negotiate drug prices under a new rule?

Signals look like
legislative proposal
regulatory guidance
government budget action
policy announcement
Core judgment question

Will the environment change?

The Clean Way to Handle This in CIOS

You do not need many more archetypes.

You need a small expansion.

Recommended Extended Archetype Set

Core clinical-commercial:

Launch Timing
Regulatory Risk
Physician Adoption
Competitive Positioning
Access / Barrier

Enterprise / system-level:

Investment / Portfolio Decision
Operational Execution / Supply Risk
Strategic Partnership / M&A
Policy / Environment Shift

That is the complete practical coverage set.

The Design Rule for Replit

Before generating signals, the system must answer:

Is this decision about:

product readiness
regulatory action
physician behavior
competitive advantage
payer access
capital allocation
operational continuity
corporate strategy
policy change

That classification determines signal grammar.
