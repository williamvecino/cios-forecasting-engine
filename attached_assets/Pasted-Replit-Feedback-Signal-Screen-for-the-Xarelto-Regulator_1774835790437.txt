Replit Feedback — Signal Screen (for the Xarelto Regulatory Case)

Case Anchor

Brand / Product
Xarelto (rivaroxaban)

Therapy Class
Direct Oral Anticoagulant (DOAC)

Decision Domain
Regulatory

Time Horizon
12 months

Strategic Question
Will the FDA implement a safety-related label change for Xarelto within the next 12 months due to GI bleeding risk signals?

Everything on this screen must be evaluated strictly against that outcome.

Right now the screen mixes relevant signals, competitor positioning, reimbursement context, and litigation noise without clearly tying them to the forecast decision.

1) Direction Labeling Is Conceptually Wrong

Current labels:

Slows label change
Neutral

These are not causal directions. They are interpretations.

Direction must represent impact on probability of the forecast outcome, not narrative tone.

Required Fix — Replace "Direction" With

Impact on Forecast

Increase probability
Decrease probability
No material impact
Ambiguous

Example Corrections

Apixaban lower GI risk study
Current:

Slows label change

Correct:

Decrease probability of label change

Xarelto lawsuits escalating
Current:

Slows label change

Correct:

Increase probability of label change

CMS excluded Xarelto from IRA negotiations
Current:

Neutral

Correct:

No material impact on label change

This single change will remove a large amount of cognitive confusion.

2) Signals Must Be Explicitly Linked to the Decision Domain

Right now signals are labeled only by source:

Scientific Publication
Press Release
News Article

But not by relevance to the regulatory decision.

Required Fix — Add a Mandatory Field

Decision Relevance

Direct regulatory evidence
Indirect regulatory signal
Market perception signal
Operational context
Not relevant

Example

Apixaban meta-analysis

Decision relevance:

Indirect regulatory signal

Lawsuits citing GI bleeding

Decision relevance:

Direct regulatory risk signal

CMS IRA negotiation exclusion

Decision relevance:

Not relevant to label change

This prevents noise from entering the forecast model.

Public perception

5) Importance and Confidence Are Being Used Without Anchors

Right now:

Importance:

High

Confidence:

Strong

But:

High relative to what?
Strong based on what?

Required Fix — Define Explicit Criteria

Importance

How much this signal could change the forecast probability

Confidence

How reliable the evidence source is

Provide Scoring Guide

Importance

Low

Changes probability <5%

Medium

Changes probability 5–15%

High

Changes probability >15%

Confidence

Weak

Unverified or anecdotal

Moderate

Single study or indirect evidence

Strong

Multiple validated sources or regulatory confirmation

Without anchors, these labels drift.

6) The Screen Needs a "Why This Signal Matters" Field

Currently missing.

Users see:

What happened

But not:

Why it changes the forecast.

Required Fix — Add

Impact Rationale

Short structured statement:

This signal increases/decreases the probability of the outcome because:

Example

Apixaban lower GI risk study

Impact rationale:

Lower bleeding risk in a competing therapy reduces pressure on regulators to modify Xarelto labeling.

Xarelto lawsuits

Impact rationale:

Legal escalation increases regulatory scrutiny and probability of safety review.

This forces causal clarity.

12) Final Structural Rule for This Screen

Every signal must answer one question:

How does this change the probability of the forecast outcome?

If it cannot answer that, it does not belong on the screen.

The Single Most Important Fix

Replace:

Direction

With:

That change alone will correct a large portion of the logic and usability issues on this page.
