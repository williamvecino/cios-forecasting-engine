Archetype 1 — Launch Timing
Example: Viatris Generic Abilify Maintena

This is your reference operational case.

Step 1 — Define Question

Question

Will Viatris launch a generic version of Abilify Maintena 1-month vial in the United States by December 31, 2026?

Comparison groups

Launch by Dec 31, 2026
Launch after Dec 31, 2026

Forecast horizon

12–24 months

Step 2 — Add Information
Primary drivers

Legal entry readiness
Regulatory readiness
Manufacturing readiness
Supply readiness

Correct signal types

Patent settlement entry window
FDA submission or approval milestone
Manufacturing slot allocation
Batch validation completion
Production capacity readiness
Packaging and labeling readiness
Inventory build progress

Incorrect signal types

Clinical superiority
Guideline inclusion
Physician adoption
Market share

Step 3 — Judge

The system must answer:

Can the product legally launch?
Can the product be approved?
Can the product be manufactured?
Can the product be supplied?

Step 4 — Decide

Output must show:

Top drivers
Top constraints
Contradictions
Probability of launch by date
Confidence
Fragility

Step 5 — Respond

Example output:

Most likely outcome: Launch in late 2026
Primary risk: Manufacturing readiness timing
Key monitor: FDA approval milestone

Step 6 — Simulate

Remove:

Manufacturing slot allocation

Expected effect:

Probability decreases materially

This is the cleanest demonstration case for CIOS.

This consistency is what makes CIOS reliable.

The Operational Rule Going Forward

Once Replit learns the five archetypes:

Every new case must first be classified into one of them.

Then the system uses the matching signal grammar.

Never mix grammars.

That is how you eliminate signal noise.
