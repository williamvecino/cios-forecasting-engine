CASE CONTEXT (for alignment)

Brand: Xarelto (rivaroxaban)
Therapy: Direct Oral Anticoagulant (DOAC)
Scenario: FDA black box warning / safety concerns around GI bleeding
Forecast question: Will safety concerns lead to regulatory restrictions within 12 months?

This is a risk / safety response case, not an adoption or launch case.
That distinction drives most of the issues observed.

SECTION 1 — Conceptual / Modeling Issues
1) Direction Logic Errors (Foundational)

Several signals were labeled with directions that contradict their real-world meaning.

Examples observed

Health economics data supporting favorable cost-effectiveness
→ labeled: Supports regulatory restrictions

Regional payer expanding formulary access
→ labeled: Supports regulatory restrictions

These are reversed.

Correct Rule

Signals must be directional to the outcome being forecast.

If the question is:

Will restrictions occur?

Then:

Signals reducing risk
→ decrease restriction probability

Signals increasing risk
→ increase restriction probability

Required Fix

Enforce directional validation logic:

Positive access signal cannot support restriction outcome

Why this matters

Direction errors corrupt:

Forecast logic
Confidence interpretation
Decision outputs

2) Double-Negative Direction Language

Example:

Decreases probability of regulatory restriction

Users interpret this as:

Increases approval

Required Fix

Use direct directional language.

Replace:

Decreases probability of regulatory restriction

With:

Reduces likelihood of restrictions

This removes cognitive translation.

3) Payer Review Slows Adoption — Incorrect Default Assumption

Observed earlier in the workflow:

Payer review → slows adoption

This is not universally true.

Correct Rule

Payer review affects:

Timing
Access conditions

Not necessarily:

Adoption intent

Required Fix

Replace default relationship:

Payer review slows adoption

With:

Payer review introduces access conditions

4) Model Treats Safety Signal as Adoption Problem

This case is fundamentally:

Risk management

But the model initially behaved like:

Market uptake forecasting

Correct framing

Safety signal → behavior shift → access tightening

Not:

Safety signal → adoption decline

5) Feasibility Within 12 Months — Misinterpreted

Observed:

Limited timeline for regulatory updates
→ treated as adoption constraint

Final Summary — Root Causes

All observed issues stem from five underlying mismatches.

Safety scenario modeled like adoption scenario
Signals labeled without directional validation
Assumptions treated like notes instead of dependencies
Core outputs displayed as secondary pop-ups
Terminology inconsistent with decision context

Fixing those five foundations resolves the majority of downstream problems.
