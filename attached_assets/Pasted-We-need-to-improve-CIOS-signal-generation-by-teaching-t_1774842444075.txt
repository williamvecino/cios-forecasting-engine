We need to improve CIOS signal generation by teaching the system how to interpret each case before searching for signals.

For every case, add a mandatory Case Framing Layer before External Signal Scout. This layer must explicitly derive:

* company
* brand / product
* forecasted outcome
* decision domain
* time horizon
* decision archetype
* allowed signal families
* forbidden signal families
* case-specific search targets

For the current Viatris case:

Case name:
Viatris generic Abilify Maintena 1-month vial U.S. launch timing

Forecast question:
Will Viatris launch a generic version of Abilify Maintena 1-month vial in the United States by December 31, 2026?

Decision archetype:
Launch timing / regulatory-manufacturing readiness

What this case is really about:
Whether legal entry, regulatory readiness, sterile manufacturing readiness, and launch operations are sufficiently advanced to support launch by the target date.

Allowed signal families:

* patent settlement / legal entry date
* ANDA filing / FDA approval / review status
* sterile facility readiness
* manufacturing slot allocation
* batch validation / CMC readiness
* production capacity allocation
* packaging / labeling / distribution readiness
* inventory build / launch stocking
* payer access / channel readiness
* internal portfolio prioritization

Forbidden signal families:

* clinical differentiation
* guideline review
* physician switching inertia
* KOL sentiment
* treatment preference
* market adoption dynamics
* standard-of-care inertia

Signal acceptance rule:
A signal is valid only if it directly changes the probability of legal, regulatory, manufacturing, or operational readiness by the target date.

Signal rejection rule:
Reject any signal that mainly affects post-launch uptake rather than launch feasibility or launch timing.

Required new behavior:

1. Generate a Decision Interpretation Block before searching
2. Use that block to constrain signal generation
3. Score each suggested signal for case relevance before display
4. Suppress suggested signals that are outside the allowed signal families

For this case, every valid signal should map to one of four questions:

1. Can Viatris legally launch by then?
2. Can Viatris get regulatory clearance by then?
3. Can Viatris manufacture enough sterile product by then?
4. Can Viatris operationally supply and distribute the product by then?

If a candidate signal does not map to one of those four questions, it should be downgraded or excluded.
